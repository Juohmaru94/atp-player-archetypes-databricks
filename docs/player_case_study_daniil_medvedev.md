# Player Case Study — Daniil Medvedev

## Overview

From 2020 to 2024, Daniil Medvedev played 330 ATP matches in the dataset and won 249 of them, for a win rate of 75.5%. His average ranking across this span was 3.6, which places him among the strongest players in the sample.

## Surface Interpretation

Medvedev’s best surface in this sample is clearly hard court, where he played the overwhelming majority of his matches and posted a 77.4% win rate. Grass was also strong, while clay was his weakest surface by a noticeable margin.

Unlike Tsitsipas, whose strongest results came on clay, Medvedev’s profile is much more aligned with fast-court success. His hard-court dominance fits well with the broader interpretation of his archetype.

## Seasonal Interpretation

Medvedev’s strongest season in this sample was 2021, where he combined elite ranking with his highest win rate. His performance remained strong in later years, but 2024 shows a decline relative to his best seasons.

A notable pattern is that his ace production declined from earlier years while double faults increased over time. That may indicate some serving-profile inefficiency in later seasons, even though he remained a top player overall.

## Evidence-Based Performance Notes

Based on this dataset, three themes stand out:

1. **Hard courts are the clearest fit for his game**
   - Hard court is both his most-played and most successful surface.
   - His results there are substantially stronger than on clay.

2. **He fits the Elite Big Server profile consistently**
   - He stayed in the same archetype in every season from 2020 to 2024.
   - His ace output and first-serve effectiveness support that classification.

3. **Later seasons show some serving tradeoff**
   - Aces declined from early-sample levels.
   - Double faults increased over time.
   - 2024 was weaker than his peak period.

## Caution
