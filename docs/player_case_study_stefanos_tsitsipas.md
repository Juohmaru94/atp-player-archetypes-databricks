# Player Case Study — Stefanos Tsitsipas

## Overview

From 2020 to 2024, Stefanos Tsitsipas played 345 ATP matches in the dataset and won 242 of them, for a win rate of 70.1%. His average ranking across this span was 6.0, which places him firmly among the top-tier players in the sample.

## Core Performance Profile

- Matches played: 345

- Average rank: 6.0
- Average aces: 7.25

- First serve in rate: 63.0%
- First serve points won rate: 76.3%
- Second serve points won rate: 55.6%
- Break points saved rate: 66.1%

## Archetype Classification

Across every season from 2020 through 2024, Tsitsipas was classified as:

**Elite Big Server**

This suggests that, in the current clustering design, his profile is consistently associated with:
- high ace production
- strong first-serve effectiveness
- solid second-serve performance
- strong overall winning results

## Surface Interpretation

Tsitsipas was most successful on clay in this dataset, where he posted a 79.6% win rate. His hard-court results were also strong, though clearly below his clay level. Grass stands out as the weakest surface in this sample, with a much lower 58.3% win rate despite the highest ace volume.

This is an interesting pattern: his serve appears most explosive on grass, but that extra ace production does not translate into his best overall results. That suggests his broader game profile may be better supported by clay than by grass.

## Seasonal Interpretation

Tsitsipas remained highly competitive across all five seasons, but there are signs of slippage in 2023 and 2024 relative to his strongest period in 2021–2022. His 2024 season stands out in particular, with both a lower win rate and a weaker average ranking than in earlier years.

His average double faults also increased in 2024 relative to prior seasons, while ace production declined slightly. That combination may point to a less efficient serving profile compared with his peak years in this sample.

## Evidence-Based Performance Notes

Based on this dataset, three themes stand out:

1. **Serve remains a major strength**
   - Tsitsipas consistently fits the Elite Big Server cluster.
   - His first-serve points won rate of 76.3% is strong.
   - His ace production is high across the full sample.

2. **Clay appears to maximize his overall results**
   - His clay win rate is substantially better than his hard-court and grass results.
   - Even without the highest ace volume, his all-around match performance appears strongest there.

3. **Recent seasons suggest some efficiency decline**
   - 2024 shows a weaker average rank than prior seasons.
   - Win rate softened relative to 2021–2022.
   - Double faults increased while aces dipped.
