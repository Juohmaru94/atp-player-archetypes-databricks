# Player Comparison — Stefanos Tsitsipas vs Daniil Medvedev

## Why this comparison matters

Tsitsipas and Medvedev were both classified as **Elite Big Server** across all seasons from 2020 to 2024. That makes them a strong comparison pair for showing that players can share the same broad archetype while still having different surface fits and performance patterns.

## Overall Comparison

| Metric | Stefanos Tsitsipas | Daniil Medvedev |
|---|---:|---:|
| Matches Played | 345 | 330 |
| Wins | 242 | 249 |
| Losses | 103 | 81 |
| Win Rate | 70.1% | 75.5% |
| Average Rank | 6.0 | 3.6 |
| Average Aces | 7.25 | 8.16 |
| Average Double Faults | 2.32 | 3.79 |
| Average Match Duration | 115.1 | 114.0 |
| First Serve In Rate | 63.0% | 63.0% |
| First Serve Win Rate | 76.3% | 76.0% |
| Second Serve Win Rate | 55.6% | 50.8% |
| Break Points Saved Rate | 66.1% | 64.2% |

## Interpretation

Both players look similar on first-serve profile, but they differ in overall performance shape:

- Medvedev won more often overall and carried the stronger average ranking.
- Medvedev generated more aces, but also many more double faults.
- Tsitsipas had stronger second-serve and break-point-save rates in this sample.
- Their first-serve metrics were remarkably close.

## Surface Comparison

| Surface | Tsitsipas Win Rate | Medvedev Win Rate |
|---|---:|---:|
| Clay | 79.6% | 65.9% |
| Grass | 58.3% | 73.7% |
| Hard | 66.3% | 77.4% |

## Surface Interpretation

This is the clearest point of separation:

- Tsitsipas performed best on clay.
- Medvedev performed best on hard court.
- Medvedev was also stronger on grass.
- Tsitsipas underperformed Medvedev on fast courts despite sharing the same broad cluster label.

This is a good example of why clustering should be treated as a high-level summary rather than a complete player identity.

## Portfolio Insight

A useful conclusion from this comparison is that **two players can share the same archetype while still requiring different surface-specific interpretations**. That makes the project stronger because it shows you are not over-interpreting cluster labels.
